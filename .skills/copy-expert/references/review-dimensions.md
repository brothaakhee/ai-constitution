# Copy Review Dimensions

Use these lenses when reviewing product messaging.

## 1. Clarity
- is the meaning immediately clear
- does the user understand what happens next
- are labels concrete instead of vague
- is jargon increasing cognitive load

## 2. Persuasion
- does the copy motivate action
- does the CTA feel specific and valuable
- is the value proposition concrete
- does the wording reduce hesitation

## 3. Trust
- does the language feel credible
- are claims appropriately supported
- does the copy reduce fear and uncertainty
- are risk, privacy, and consequences explained when needed

## 4. Brand Voice
- does the tone feel deliberate and consistent
- does the voice fit the audience and product category
- is the product differentiated or generic
- does the wording sound confident without sounding inflated

## 5. Density
- is there too much copy for the moment
- is there too little context for user confidence
- are instructions concise enough
- does the screen feel text-heavy or under-explained

## 6. Psychological Friction
- what might make the user hesitate
- what wording creates doubt or resistance
- where is the emotional tone mismatched to the action
- where does the messaging fail to reassure, orient, or motivate

## Severity Guidance
- High: likely harms trust, blocks comprehension, or significantly hurts conversion
- Medium: creates meaningful hesitation, ambiguity, or weakens positioning
- Low: polish issue or mild inefficiency in wording
